🧪 Theory

Isoelectric Focusing

• Amino Acid that is found in protein has a net charge of 0 due to the presence of + and - charge.
• Iso-Electric pH of protein is the pH at which net charge on protein becomes 0 At this point the protein will not migrate in Electric Field
• Hence, we can make use of pH gradient for the separation of different protein bases on the Isoelectric point.
• This technique is what is known as Isoelectric Focusing and 2D gel electrophoresis.
• For IEF, a gel with pH gradient is made for the experiment.
• Proteins are loaded at the end of the gel with the lowest pH
• Protein will migrate, to the point where the Isoelectric Point Is equivalent to the pH of the gel.
• It will in turn obtain an equal number of + and - charge. Hence Net charge becomes 0 and stops migrating.
• Migration will stop when pH = pI. This is the basis of separation in IEF

2D Gel Electrophoresis

•IEF is usually followed by 2D gel electrophoresis, also known as SDS-PAGE
• It further allows the separation of protein.
• It is widely used to find the presence of protein, expression protein or the identification in different cells or tissue.
